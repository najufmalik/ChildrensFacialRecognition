Children's Facial Recognition


From an early age and throughout one’s lifespan, emotional skills are essential to communicate our emotions to others and adapt our behavior according to both our internal feelings and the reactions of those around us. Understanding how we feel and showing empathy towards others are essential skills for individuals of any age. Visual signals, such as facial expressions, are crucial components for people to understand as emotional signals. For children, understanding facial expressions and developing empathy for others are crucial for their social-communication development. However, children with Autism Spectrum Conditions (ASC) have major difficulties in detecting and recognizing the emotional and mental states of other people's facial expressions (Baron-Cohen).

The problem that we are trying to tackle involves deducing human emotions, but specifically, detecting what facial expressions represent in terms of how an individual may feel is not considered an easy task. Our focus will be on specifically assisting children with Autism Spectrum Conditions (ASC) to learn how to detect facial expressions in their parents and peers of all races and genders. While our focus is on children, anyone would be able to use this to learn about facial expressions and what they represent.

Why is this problem important?


Emotion recognition can be applied in many different situations. Being able to detect facial expressions can give an insight into how people may be feeling, without any speech or actions involved. For children, especially those with Autism Spectrum Conditions (ASC), recognizing facial expressions can be very difficult, and not building this skill early on can potentially stunt their ability to form social skills and relationships later in life

What is the current state-of-the-art?


The current state of the art for facial expression detection involves machine learning algorithms. There are many different kinds of algorithms that attempt to do facial detection. There are several examples of these algorithms such as knowledge-based detection, template matching, and feature-based detection. Knowledge-based detection is where there are rules defined in order to parse individual’s facial features. Template matching is more self explanitory where they use predefined templates to compare facial details, especially focusing on edge detection. Feature-based detection extracts structural features of the face and attempts to parse facial landmarks as emotions are largely shown through the eyes, eyebrows, lips, and other movements in the face. Facial detection algorithms aren’t limited to these I listed, however they are commonly used approaches when it comes to putting a face together. Covariance pooling is also used among learning algorithms to determine where the facial landmarks are located on an individual's face, while dimensionality reduction helps reduce the “noise” in the face that isn’t relevant to determine what type of emotion is recognized. There are many different ways to approach facial expression detection and each method comes with its pros and cons. Many algorithms have fatal flaws when it comes to things like variance in lighting, skin color or angle that the image was taken from. We are trying to use an implementation that works especially with the change in skin color or lighting as we plan to focus on a diverse subset of individuals to test on.
